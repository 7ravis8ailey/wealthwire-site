## What "Bad Credit" Means for Card Applications

Credit scores below **580** are generally considered "bad" by FICO standards. Below 500 is very poor. In that range:
- Most unsecured cards will decline you
- Secured cards (with a deposit) become the main option
- Your goal isn't rewards — it's rebuilding your score so you can access better products

The cards below are designed for scores in the 300–580 range, or for people with no credit history at all.

## The Cards to Avoid

Some secured and "bad credit" cards are designed to profit from your limited options:

❌ **Cards with monthly fees** — some charge $5–$10/month on top of an annual fee
❌ **Cards that take your deposit as "processing" fees** — your deposit should be a refundable security deposit, not a fee
❌ **Cards with extremely low limits relative to their fees** — a $300 limit with a $75 annual fee means your card is 25% eaten by fees on day one
❌ **Cards that don't report to all 3 bureaus** — if they don't report to all three, they can't build your credit properly
