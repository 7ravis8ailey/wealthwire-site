## 3 Ways to Waive the Fee at Your Current Bank

If you don't want to switch banks yet, most maintenance fees can be waived by meeting one of these conditions:

### 1. Set Up Direct Deposit
This is the easiest waiver at most banks. Route your paycheck to your checking account and the fee disappears — even a small recurring deposit often qualifies.

- **Chase Total Checking:** Fee waived with any direct deposit
- **Wells Fargo Everyday Checking:** Fee waived with $500/month direct deposit
- **Bank of America Advantage:** Fee waived with $250/month direct deposit

### 2. Maintain a Minimum Balance
Most banks waive the fee if you keep a set minimum in the account.

- **Chase:** $1,500 daily minimum
- **Wells Fargo:** $500 daily minimum
- **BofA:** $1,500 minimum balance

The catch: that's money sitting idle earning nothing. A $1,500 minimum at 0.01% APY earns you $0.15/year. Put it in a high-yield savings account and earn $60+.

### 3. Ask Them to Waive It
Seriously — call your bank and ask. Customer retention reps have discretion to waive fees, especially if you've been a customer for years. The script: *"I noticed a monthly maintenance fee on my account. I'd like to have that waived going forward — can you help me with that?"*

Works more often than you'd think.

## Why Banks Charge These Fees
