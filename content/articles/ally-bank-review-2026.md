## What Ally Gets Right

### 1. Consistently Strong Savings Rate — No Strings
Ally's High Yield Savings Account earns **3.20% APY** with no minimum balance and no requirements. You don't need direct deposit. You don't need to maintain a balance. You don't need to do anything except deposit money.

This "no conditions" approach sets Ally apart. SoFi's higher 4% rate requires direct deposit — without it, SoFi drops to 1.20%. Ally stays at 3.20% regardless.

For people without consistent direct deposit or who park savings separately from their paycheck account, Ally is often the best rate available.

### 2. CoverDraft — Best Free Overdraft Protection
Ally's CoverDraft program provides **up to $250 in free overdraft coverage** on debit card purchases. No opt-in required once you've qualified. No fee when you use it.

To qualify: maintain a consistent account history and have at least one direct deposit. Once active, your debit card transactions up to $250 will go through even when you're short.

$250 is the highest free overdraft limit among the top no-fee banks.

### 3. No Fees — Genuinely None
No monthly fee. No minimum balance fee. No overdraft fee. No incoming wire fee. No stop payment fee. No card replacement fee.

Ally published a one-page fee schedule. Most banks publish several pages.

### 4. Out-of-Network ATM Reimbursement
Ally reimburses up to **$10/month** in out-of-network ATM fees. Plus 43,000+ free Allpoint ATMs. This is important for frequent travelers or people in areas without Allpoint ATMs.

### 5. Buckets — Savings Organization Built In
Ally's savings account lets you create "buckets" within a single account — so you can save for a vacation, emergency fund, and car repair all in the same account without opening multiple accounts. Tracks balances separately, earns the same rate across all.

### 6. Ally Invest — Seamlessly Integrated
Ally's brokerage is built into the same app. Move money from savings to investments instantly. If you want to keep everything at one institution, Ally covers banking, savings, and investing.

## Where Ally Falls Short

### No Early Direct Deposit
Ally doesn't release your paycheck early. Chime and SoFi both offer 2-day early direct deposit. If getting paid early matters to you, Ally isn't the right pick.

### No Cash Deposits
Ally has no way to accept cash deposits. If you get paid in cash or regularly need to deposit physical money, you'll need a secondary account at a bank or credit union.

### Savings Rate Below SoFi (With Direct Deposit)
If you have consistent direct deposit, SoFi's 4%+ rate beats Ally's 3.20%. The gap is ~$80/year on a $10,000 balance. Worth noting for heavy savers.

### No Credit-Building Tools
Unlike Chime (Credit Builder card) or SoFi (credit monitoring + credit builder loans), Ally doesn't offer any tools for improving your credit score.
