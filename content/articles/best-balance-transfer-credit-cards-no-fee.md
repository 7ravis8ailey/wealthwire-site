## How Balance Transfers Work

1. Apply for a balance transfer card
2. Request a transfer of your existing balance to the new card
3. Pay 0% interest for the intro period (12–21 months)
4. Pay off the balance before the intro period ends

Most cards charge a **3–5% balance transfer fee** on the amount transferred. On a $5,000 balance, that's $150–$250 — but you'll save thousands in interest, so it's almost always worth it.

## The 5 Rules of Balance Transfers

1. **Stop using the old card.** You just got rid of the debt — don't add to it.
2. **Make every payment on time.** One missed payment can void your 0% APR on some cards.
3. **Calculate your monthly payoff amount.** Divide your balance by the number of 0% months. That's your minimum target payment.
4. **Don't apply for other credit while you're paying this off.** Hard inquiries hurt during this window.
5. **Have a plan for when the 0% period ends.** Either pay it off, or transfer again.
