## What Is a Secured Credit Card?

A secured card requires a refundable deposit (usually $200–$500) that becomes your credit limit. You use the card like a normal credit card and pay it monthly. The issuer reports your payment history to the three major credit bureaus — that's how you build credit.

The deposit is **fully refunded** when you close the account or upgrade to an unsecured card.

## How Fast Can You Build Credit With a Secured Card?

With consistent on-time payments and low utilization (keep your balance under 30% of your limit), most people see meaningful credit score improvement within **3–6 months.** Scores often move from the 500s to the 600s in 6 months, and 700+ within 12–18 months.

**The only things that matter:**
1. Pay on time, every month (set up autopay for the minimum)
2. Keep utilization low (under 30% of your limit)
3. Don't apply for a bunch of other credit at the same time

## What to Avoid

**Cards with annual fees:** You shouldn't pay $49–$75 per year to rebuild your credit. Every card above is free.

**Cards with high APR + fees:** Some predatory secured cards charge annual fees, monthly fees, and credit limit fees that eat most of your available credit. Avoid any secured card with a monthly maintenance fee.

**Cards that don't report to all 3 bureaus:** Make sure your card reports to Equifax, Experian, AND TransUnion. All the cards above do.
